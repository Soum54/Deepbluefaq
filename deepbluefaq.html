Basic Questions

What is the type of commodity or cargo being shipped?
This could vary widely depending on the shipment, e.g., electronics, perishable goods, machinery, textiles, or raw materials. The commodity must comply with international shipping and safety regulations.
What is the HS code for the commodity?
The Harmonized System (HS) code depends on the specific commodity. For example, electronic components might have an HS code starting with "85." You can consult the official HS code database or your local customs authority for the exact code.
What is the origin and destination country?
Example: The shipment originates from India and is destined for the United States.
What is the size of the cartons or boxes?
Example: Each box measures 50x50x50 cm.
What is the weight and volume of the shipment?
Example: The shipment weighs 500 kg with a total volume of 2 cubic meters.
What is the preferred mode of freight shipping (air, sea, or land)?
Example: Air freight for faster delivery, or sea freight for cost-effectiveness on bulk shipments.
Do you need port-to-port or door-to-door delivery?
Example: Door-to-door delivery for convenience and end-to-end logistics.
What type of freight is it (LTL, FTL, or parcel)?
Example: Less than Truckload (LTL) for small shipments, Full Truckload (FTL) for larger volumes, or parcel for smaller individual packages.
Do you have the required licenses to import or export the goods?
Example: Yes, we have the necessary export license issued by [country-specific authority] and an import license for the destination country.
What is the transit time, and how is it calculated?
Example: Transit time is estimated at 10-12 days by sea freight. It is calculated based on the distance between the origin and destination ports, type of vessel, and customs clearance time.
What documents are needed for international shipping?
Commonly required documents include:
  • Commercial invoice
  • Packing list
  • Bill of lading (BOL)
  • Certificate of origin
  • Import/export licenses
  • Insurance certificate
  • Customs declaration form
Are duties and taxes included, and how are they calculated?
Example: Duties and taxes are not included and are calculated based on the shipment's declared value, HS code, and specific country tariffs. These are typically paid by the importer unless otherwise agreed.
